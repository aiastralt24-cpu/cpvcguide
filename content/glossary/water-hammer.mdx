## Definition

Water hammer is a pressure-shock event caused by a rapid change in flow, such as a valve closing quickly. It is not just a noise word. It describes a real system event that can add stress to fittings, supports, and weak points in the line.

## Why readers should care

This term is important because many people hear the sound, notice the shock, and then blame only the pipe material. In reality, the system event matters too. A plumbing line reacts to sudden pressure changes, not only to steady-state conditions.

## The common mistake

The common mistake is treating water hammer as only an annoyance. The noise is often the visible sign, but the real issue is the repeated pressure shock behind it.

## What to do next

If you are seeing cracked fittings, noisy closures, or thread-related stress problems, use this term as part of the diagnosis. Then move back to the troubleshooting page and check whether the system behavior is contributing to the failure.
