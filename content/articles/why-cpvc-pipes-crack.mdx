## Start with the root-cause mindset

Cracks are symptoms, not conclusions. A useful troubleshooting page should slow the reader down and separate the visible break from the operating or installation condition that created it.

## Common contributing factors

Poor support, concentrated stress, exposure to conditions outside the design envelope, and weak transition details are typical factors. The strongest explanation is rarely just one word like brittle.

## Why diagnosis matters before repair

If the system is repaired without correcting the cause, the same condition can reappear elsewhere. Troubleshooting content should therefore guide readers from symptom to investigation, not just from symptom to product replacement.
