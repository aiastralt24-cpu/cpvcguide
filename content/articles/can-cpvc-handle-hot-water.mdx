## The short answer

CPVC is commonly used for hot-water plumbing because the material is positioned for elevated domestic water temperatures, but that does not eliminate the need for proper design and installation.

## What makes the answer more complex

The hot-water question changes shape depending on where the pipe is used. A bathroom branch line, a heater-adjacent connection, and a system with repeated temperature swings all create different demands on the piping.

## Where designers and installers should pay attention

The main checkpoints are maximum expected temperature, pressure at that temperature, transition details near equipment, and how thermal movement is managed along the run. A category page that simply says yes or no is not doing enough work.

## When the answer may become no

If the system is better described as a steam, boiler, or uncontrolled heat application, the discussion moves beyond a normal domestic hot-water decision. That is where exclusion pages matter.
